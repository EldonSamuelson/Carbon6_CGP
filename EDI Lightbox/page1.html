Environmental Deprivation Index

Our carbon sink quality environmental deprivation index (EDI) is based on 8 variables (shown in the pie chart). The EDI was developed to identify which areas of Edinburgh are the most deprived of carbon sequestrating vegetation and should be targeted for improvements.

All zones are ranked on a scale of 0-10, where 0 is the most deprived and 10 is the least.

The Variables

% High Carbon Sequestering Land Cover: Dense canopy cover (>200 t/ha carbon stored) estimated from remotely sensed data and fieldwork.

% Medium Carbon Sequestering Land Cover: Scattered canopy cover (50-200 t/ha carbon stored) estimated from remotely sensed data and fieldwork.

% Woodland Cover: Recognised by the national forest inventory. Woodland is > 0.5 ha and minimum 20% canopy cover.

Population Density: Population/ ha. Areas with lower population densities likely have more space to sequester carbon.

The following variables were created from Edinburgh Council records of species, age and size of all trees on public land in Edinburgh.

Species Diversity: The number of species present relative to the number of trees in each zone.

Juvenile Trees/ ha: A higher count of juvenile trees suggests more carbon will be sequestered as these trees grow.

Trees/ ha: Trees are a great store of carbon. A greater number of trees means more carbon is stored.

Mean DBH: Average diameter at breast height indicates size of trees within a zone. Larger trees are able to store more carbon.
